Why Not Just Accuracy?
With ~16% deterioration prevalence, a trivially model that always predicts "stable" achieves 84% accuracy but sensitivity = 0% — missing every deteriorating patient. We use:
AUROCThreshold-agnostic discrimination
AUPRCGold-standard for imbalanced classes
SENSMinimise missed deteriorating patients
NPVSafety of clearing a patient as stable

Method
Permutation Importance: each feature is shuffled across 20 repeats; AUROC drop is measured. Large drop = critical feature. Mathematically equivalent to SHAP TreeExplainer global rankings.

Key insight: Engineered features (slopes, shock index) appear alongside raw vitals in the top-10, validating the feature engineering hypothesis — trajectories matter as much as absolute values.

10 Engineered Feature Families
F1 · Latest Values
Most recent vital reading. Captures current physiological state.
F2 · Trend Slopes
Linear regression slope over 6-hour window. Catches rising HR / falling SpO₂ before thresholds are breached.
F3 · Variability (Std)
Intra-patient standard deviation over monitoring window. High variability signals autonomic instability.
F4 · Rolling Range
Max − min over last 4 observations (2 hrs). Sensitive to acute decompensation events.
F5 · NEWS2 Score
Validated RCP NEWS2 rubric as a feature — model builds upon clinical wisdom.
F6 · Missingness %
Sicker patients have more monitoring gaps — missingness itself is a clinical signal.
F7 · Demographics
Age + Charlson comorbidity score. Established independent predictors of mortality.
F8 · Shock Index
HR ÷ SBP. Value >0.9 = haemodynamic instability. Validated occult shock marker.
F9 · Pulse Pressure
SBP − DBP. Narrow <25 → tamponade. Wide >100 → sepsis vasodilation.
F10 · HR/RR Ratio
Autonomic imbalance marker. Co-occurring tachycardia + tachypnoea = sentinel sepsis sign.
Deployment Note
Production monitoring · Failure modes · Human oversight requirements
Rule-Based Alert Thresholds
🟢
GREEN
Risk < 0.30 AND NEWS < 5
Routine 4-hourly observations. No escalation required.
🟡
AMBER
Risk 0.30–0.80 OR NEWS 5–6
Hourly obs. Nurse-in-charge. Senior review within 30 min.
🔴
RED
Risk ≥ 0.80 OR NEWS ≥ 7 (override)
IMMEDIATE MET call. Continuous monitoring. ICU consult.
Hard Override: NEWS ≥ 7 always fires RED regardless of ML score — preserving the validated clinical rule as a patient-safety net.
Monitoring Strategy in Production
📊
Data Drift Monitoring
  • Weekly KS-test on incoming vital distributions vs training baseline. Alert if p < 0.01.
  • Monthly feature importance re-run. Material shifts trigger revalidation.
  • Flag records with >20% missing vitals per window.
📈
Prediction Monitoring
  • Track daily GREEN/AMBER/RED distribution. Sudden changes → drift audit.
  • Monthly calibration check (isotonic regression, predicted vs actual).
  • Track false-positive rate via downstream outcome linkage.
🔗
Outcome Linkage
  • Link predictions to ICU transfers, rapid response calls, in-hospital mortality.
  • Quarterly sensitivity/specificity re-computation with labelled outcomes.
  • Retraining trigger: sensitivity < 0.80 OR AUROC < 0.90.
🔄
Retraining Protocol
  • Quarterly retraining on rolling 12-month data window.
  • Offline validation on held-out test set before production deployment.
  • Keep previous model in shadow mode — rapid rollback within 1 hour.
Failure Modes — Clinicians Must Watch For
⚠ Silent Model Degradation
The model may degrade silently with unchanged confidence but increasing error rates. If clinical intuition conflicts with the score, always escalate on clinical grounds.
⚠ Distributional Shift
Seasonal surges (dengue/influenza), ward reconfigurations, or demographic shifts move vital distributions away from training data. Unexplained spikes in AMBER/RED alerts must trigger an immediate audit.
⚠ Missing Data Cascade
If a monitoring device fails, the model substitutes medians — clinically misleading for genuinely ill patients. GREEN alerts with >30% missing vitals must be treated with heightened suspicion.
⚠ Alert Fatigue
If AMBER alerts exceed 15% of daily observations without confirmed clinical need, raise the threshold and notify the governance committee. Alert fatigue causes nurses to ignore genuine alerts.
⚠ Demographic Gaps
Paediatric patients, post-surgical patients, and those with chronic baseline abnormalities (e.g., COPD with SpO₂ 91% baseline) are outside training distribution — validate separately before deployment to those wards.
Human Oversight — Non-Negotiable
🩺
Clinical Veto
Any clinician may escalate or de-escalate regardless of ML alert. The model is advisory; the clinician is accountable. This must be policy, not just guidance.
⚖️
Ethics & Equity Audit
Quarterly audit of performance across age, sex, diagnosis groups. ML models can encode and amplify historical biases in care delivery.
📋
Living Model Card
Training data provenance, known limitations, demographic gaps, last validation date — visible to all clinical staff, reviewed by Clinical AI Governance Committee quarterly.
🚨
Incident Reporting
Any case where model output contributed to harm must be reported — identical to drug incident reporting. No exceptions.
